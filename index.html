The Roles

Who We're Casting

Five roles, reviewed on a rolling basis. Read the role, record a self-tape, and submit below.

COREY Lead Extended Role

Male · plays 30s

A grieving father and husband, pushed past his limit. Carries the film almost entirely alone through a tense first half before the story turns hard in its second. Heavy emotional and physical role, including a staged, rehearsed fight and a major tonal shift late in the script.

We're looking for: strong dramatic range, believable vulnerability, and the ability to flip to something dangerous. Comfort with physical / stunt-style choreography (padded, safety first).

MASKED MAN Physical / Stunt Role

Any gender · athletic build

Masked for the entirety of a pivotal, physically demanding scene. Performs a staged fight. Cast to be a close physical match for whoever plays Corey — similar height, build, and hair.

We're looking for: a physical performer comfortable with staged action. Please tell us your height, build, and hair.

BRAD Supporting

Male · plays 30s–40s

Corey's warm, worried brother. Appears in one intercut phone-call sequence. Small role, low time commitment, can be shot separately.

We're looking for: warmth and natural, grounded delivery in a phone conversation.

WIFE Supporting One shoot day

Female · plays 30s

Held captive and terrified. Primarily reaction and screams, physically constrained in the scene.

We're looking for: committed, believable fear and strong reaction work.

DAUGHTER Supporting One shoot day

Adult (18+) who reads young

A young captive, terrified. Reaction and screams, plus one off-screen line.

We're looking for: strong reaction and scream work.

This role is cast with an adult performer only. We are not casting anyone under 18.

The Process

How To Submit

  1. Record your audition on any phone or camera using the guidelines beside this.
  2. Upload it to YouTube (unlisted), Google Drive, or Vimeo, set so anyone with the link can view.
  3. Fill out the form below and paste your video link. We review on a rolling basis.
  4. Selected performers get a short live video callback and an availability check.

Sides to perform: Download the audition sides (PDF)

Self-Tape Guidelines

Make It Count

  • Slate first. Start by saying your name, height, and the role you're reading for.
  • Shoot horizontal (landscape), framed chest-up, plain background.
  • Light your face evenly in a quiet, well-lit space.
  • Clear audio matters more than a fancy camera. Record somewhere quiet.
  • Use a reader off-camera for the other lines if you can.
  • Keep it to the sides. A short second take is welcome.
  • Test your link before submitting. Make sure it isn't set to private.

The Crew

Who We Need Behind the Camera

Five positions across three days. Volunteer crew — credit, meals, and clips for your reel. All equipment is provided — you're welcome to work with your own kit if you'd rather, but nothing is required. Tell us what you've worked on.

SOUND MIXER / BOOM OP 1 opening Gear provided

All three days · Fri–Sun

One person running production sound end to end — booming and recording. Two of the three days are exterior and after dark, so wind and road noise are the real challenge. The third is an interior day in a basement, with a physically demanding scene and multiple actors.

We're looking for: someone comfortable working solo and monitoring their own levels. We supply everything — recorder, boom, mics, and wireless lavs — so you can plant mics for the physical scenes instead of chasing them on a boom. Bring your own kit if you prefer, but you don't need to own anything.

GAFFER / KEY GRIP 1 opening Gear provided

All three days · Fri–Sun

Every scene in the film is night, so lighting is doing most of the work. You'd design and run lighting across three setups — a hilltop exterior, an unfinished basement, and a house exterior — plus stands, flags, negative fill, and one slider move. You'd also own power: we're running off a generator at the exterior location.

We're looking for: someone who can light a night exterior with limited units and knows their way around a generator, distro, and safe cable runs. Lights, stands, grip, and power are all supplied — bring your own if you like, but it isn't required.

PRODUCTION ASSISTANT 2 openings No experience required

All three days preferred · Fri–Sun

The most useful people on a small night shoot. Lockups and keeping the set quiet, hauling gear up and down basement stairs, watching for traffic and neighbors at the exterior locations, food runs, and resets between takes.

We're looking for: reliable, awake, and happy to be outside overnight. No experience needed — this is a good first film credit.

SFX MAKEUP / BLOOD 1 opening Materials provided

Shoot Saturday · plus one prep session beforehand

One day, one room, a lot of blood. A staged fight that escalates in stages across roughly eight hours, with two more performers held in continuity makeup the whole time. You'd own the blood plan — how much, where it goes, and keeping it consistent once it starts, because it only ever goes one direction.

We're looking for: someone comfortable with practical blood and, just as much, with tracking continuity across a long day. Prosthetics aren't required. We'd also want you for a blood test in the week before the shoot — testing the recipe on camera, on skin, wardrobe and walls, and confirming cleanup works on the real surfaces. We supply the materials and cover the budget.

What we're offering: credit · meals every shoot day · clips for your reel. All equipment provided. These are unpaid volunteer positions.

What to expect: three consecutive days, Friday–Sunday, Aug 21–23, 2026, in the Lafayette, Indiana area. Friday and Sunday are overnight calls — evening start, wrapping around 5 AM. Saturday starts at 2 PM and wraps around 1:30 AM. Horror content includes a staged fight and practical stage blood.
